What I Found On Your Site

01
✗

Nearly the entire site is indexed on insecure http://

Every page Google has indexed for naequestrian.co.uk loads over plain http:// — except one inner page (Local/Littleborough/Lessons-Activities.php), which is oddly indexed on https:// instead. That mismatch means most visitors see a browser "Not Secure" warning before they even see your lesson prices.

02
⚠

Five nearly identical pages are competing with each other

Separate pages built for Stacksteads, Whitworth, Littleborough, Rochdale and Rawtenstall all carry almost the same title — "NA Equestrian | Riding School and Experienced Service [Town]" — a pattern that usually dilutes ranking instead of helping it.

03
⚠

A second, unconnected site exists with a different contact email

A separate Wix microsite (nikiashworth.wixsite.com/naequestrian) lists nikiashworth@hotmail.co.uk, while your main site's Contact page lists naequestrian@outlook.com — two names, two emails, for the same riding school.

The Single Biggest Opportunity

One secure site, one clear way to get in touch.

Right now your site shows a browser security warning, and a second unconnected site lists a different contact email entirely. Moving to https://, consolidating the five town pages into one real local-SEO page, and retiring or linking the Wix site removes the confusion and the security warning in one pass.

What I'd Fix In The First Week

  1. 01Install an SSL certificate and force https:// sitewide so the "Not Secure" warning disappears.
  2. 02Merge the five near-duplicate town pages into one strong "Riding Lessons near Rossendale" page listing every town you serve.
  3. 03Redirect or clearly link the Wix microsite to the main domain so there's one authoritative NA Equestrian online.
  4. 04Standardize on one contact email everywhere so enquiries never go to the wrong inbox.
  5. 05Add LocalBusiness schema with ABRS approval so Bacup-area searchers see it as a rich result.
