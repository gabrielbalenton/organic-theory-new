What I Found On Your Site

01
⚠

Your homepage isn't targeting the searches your specialties should win

When someone searches "psychiatrist Palm Coast FL" or "Spravato Palm Coast," those specialty treatments are exactly what should surface — but a broad tagline like "Mental Wellness in Palm Coast, FL" doesn't give Google specific, indexable pages to rank for the searches that would actually bring in new patients.

02
⚠

The "Award Winning Practice" badge isn't doing any work

It's a genuinely strong trust signal — but if it isn't explained anywhere (which award, from whom, what year), it's not building the credibility it could be. Right now a visitor, and Google, has to take it on faith.

03
✗

Your biggest differentiator isn't leading the page

You're one of the few practices in the area offering Saturday hours (1st & 3rd, 11am–4pm) alongside telehealth — a real scheduling advantage for working parents and anyone who can't take a weekday off. That's not what leads the homepage today, and it's exactly the kind of detail that belongs in your page title, not buried in the hours.

04
✗

No bridge between the website and your social growth goal

You're aiming to grow from around 150 Instagram/Facebook followers to 1,000+ in six months — but there's no visible connection between the site and those accounts today. No embedded feed, no content pulling website visitors toward the pages you're trying to grow, and no reason for a follower to click back to the site.

What I'd Fix In The First 2 Weeks

  1. 01Rewrite the homepage hero and meta description to foreground weekend availability, telehealth, and Spravato/IV ketamine as searchable specialties.
  2. 02Add MedicalBusiness/Physician schema markup so Google can show hours, services, and — once connected — your existing reviews as rich results.
  3. 03Build dedicated, indexable pages for high-intent searches — "Spravato Palm Coast," "IV ketamine therapy Palm Coast," "ADHD testing Palm Coast."
  4. 04Explain and link the "Award Winning Practice" badge so it actually builds credibility instead of just sitting there.
