The Single Biggest Opportunity

One clean homepage, no stray test listings.

A pottery family with a real, 40-year story — Germany to a 100-year-old Michigan homestead — is currently showing up in Google with a placeholder-titled test product still live, and a homepage split across two indexed addresses under a title that says nothing about the craft or the family. None of this needs new content — it's cleanup of what's already there, and it's the kind of thing a first-time searcher notices in about two seconds.

What I'd Fix In The First 2-3 Weeks

  1. 01Remove or noindex the "lkj;lkjfjas m," test product listing so it stops showing up publicly.
  2. 02Rewrite the homepage title to lead with "Otis Pottery," "handmade stoneware," and "East Jordan, Michigan."
  3. 03Set a canonical tag pointing /pages/frontpage to the root domain so only one homepage address is indexed.
  4. 04Add LocalBusiness/Artisan schema with the East Jordan address so Google has one clean, structured source of truth.
