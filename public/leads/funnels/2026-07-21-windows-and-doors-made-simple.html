The Single Biggest Opportunity

Titles that describe the page, not repeat your phone number.

A licensed, family-owned contractor with 26 years in the Twin Cities is currently showing up in Google with a phone number and email stapled onto every single page title, plus at least one URL running past 200 characters of stacked keywords. None of this needs new content — it's title and URL cleanup that lets what's already true about the business (licensed, local, decades of experience) actually stand out in search results.

What I'd Fix In The First 2-3 Weeks

  1. 01Rewrite every page title to describe what's on the page, moving phone and email into the page body and structured data instead of the title tag.
  2. 02Shorten the keyword-stuffed window replacement URL to a clean, readable slug, with a redirect from the old address.
  3. 03Add LocalBusiness schema naming Stout Construction, the license number, and the Minnetonka service area.
  4. 04Add unique meta descriptions per service page so each one can compete for its own specific search, not the same generic snippet.
