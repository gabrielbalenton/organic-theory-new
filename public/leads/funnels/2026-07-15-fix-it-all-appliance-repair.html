Growth Audit — Fix-It-All

Your trust signals are borrowed from someone else's server.

Fix-It-All has 15+ years as an authorised Bosch, Gaggenau, V-Zug, Kleenmaid, Neff and Siemens service agent in Melbourne — real credibility. But the brand logos proving that, and your homepage's search listing, aren't set up to hold onto it.

What I Found On Your Site

01
✗

Your authorised-brand logos are hotlinked from a third party's demo server

The Gaggenau, Kleenmaid, V-Zug, Siemens, Bosch and Neff logos on your homepage — the exact proof that backs up "authorised service agent" — are loaded directly from demo5.netwizarddesign.com.au, your web developer's staging site, not fixitall.net.au. If that demo server is ever taken down or reorganized, every one of those trust logos disappears from your page with no warning.

02
✗

No meta description on your homepage

Your homepage doesn't have one at all. When someone searches "Bosch appliance repair Melbourne," Google has nothing specific to pull for the results snippet — it'll auto-generate something generic instead of your actual 15-year track record.

03
⚠

Your title tag just says "Fix-it All" — no location, no service

The browser tab and Google search title for your homepage reads only "Fix-it All." No "Melbourne," no "appliance repair," nothing that tells Google (or a searcher scanning results) what you do or where — even though you've served the northern suburbs for 15+ years.

04
⚠

Real 5-star reviews are on the page, but not reinforced by schema

You've got genuinely great Google reviews already embedded — Simone, Craig, and the team clearly do excellent work. But without structured data tied to those reviews, Google can't show your star rating directly in search results the way it could.
