The Single Biggest Opportunity

Two page titles, rewritten to say what's actually there.

A bookstore that's been part of Anacortes for 35+ years has a real, standing Book Club — but the page for it is indexed on Google as "General 2." Its blog carries the same leftover placeholder pattern as "Blog 3." Renaming both titles to say what's actually on the page is minutes of work that directly affects whether someone searching for your book club, or your blog, ever finds it.

What I'd Fix In The First 2-3 Weeks

  1. 01Rename the /book-club page title from "General 2" to something real — "Book Club — Watermark Book Company, Anacortes WA."
  2. 02Rename the /blog-3-1 page title from "Blog 3" to something descriptive of what the blog actually covers.
  3. 03Check the rest of the site for other numbered placeholder titles left over from the same page-building process, and rename those too.
  4. 04Write real meta descriptions for the Book Club and blog pages so each one earns its own click from search, instead of a blank or generic line.
