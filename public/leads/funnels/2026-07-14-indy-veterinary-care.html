Growth Audit — Indy Veterinary Care

Your homepage names every neighborhood you serve — four separate times.

Indy Veterinary Care is a genuinely independent, family-run practice with real client trust behind it — but the homepage copy repeats the same list of Philly neighborhoods so many times it reads like keyword-stuffing, which is exactly the kind of thin, duplicated content that can hurt you in search rather than help.

What I Found On Your Site

01
⚠

The same neighborhood list, repeated four times

Northern Liberties, Fishtown, Old City, Society Hill, South Street, Olde Kensington, Queen Village, South Jersey — that exact list, reworded slightly each time, shows up in at least four separate paragraphs on your homepage: "Are you looking for the best veterinary clinic in Philadelphia? Or perhaps in South Jersey?", then again for "Whether you have dogs or cats, live in Fishtown, Old City, Society Hill...", then again for "Our patients travel to us... including Northern Liberties, Fishtown, Old City...". To a real visitor it reads unnaturally repetitive. To Google, it can look like thin, duplicative content — the opposite of what actually helps local SEO.

02
✗

Your own service pages aren't linked from that copy

You've already built individual service pages — like /pet-dentist-in-philadelphia/ — that could carry location and service targeting on their own. But the homepage prose doesn't link out to them; it just keeps repeating the same neighborhood names in paragraph form instead of pointing to the pages built to rank for them.

03
✗

Real reviews on your homepage aren't marked up for search

You already have genuine reviews from real clients — Sarah, Micel, Sean, Virginia — praising Dr. Sara and Dr. Organist by name. That's rare, valuable social proof most clinics don't have. But there's no review schema behind it, so none of that trust actually reaches the Google search result itself.

04
⚠

No single clear line about what makes you different

"Independent, family-run, more personal than the corporate chains" and "a low-stress environment" are genuinely strong differentiators — but they're buried inside the repetitive location paragraphs instead of leading the page. A Fishtown pet owner scanning search results has no fast way to see why you're not just another clinic.

The Single Biggest Opportunity

Rewrite the homepage copy once, add schema for your real reviews, and let your existing service pages carry the location-targeting instead of repeating neighborhood names four times.

Independent practices win on trust and personal care — you already have both, and real reviews to prove it. Closing the technical gap (schema, cross-linking) and the messaging gap (one clean rewrite instead of four repeats) together is what moves you past the corporate chains in local search.

What I'd Fix In The First 2 Weeks

  1. 01Rewrite the homepage copy once, clearly and warmly — remove the four repeated neighborhood mentions.
  2. 02Link the homepage to your existing service pages (like /pet-dentist-in-philadelphia/) instead of repeating what they already say.
  3. 03Add VeterinaryCare + LocalBusiness schema with your real address, phone, and services.
  4. 04Mark up your real reviews — from clients like Sarah, Micel, Sean, and Virginia — with review schema so they can show as star ratings in search.
  5. 05Add descriptive alt text to every clinic and patient photo.
