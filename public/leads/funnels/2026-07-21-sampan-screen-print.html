The Single Biggest Opportunity

Titles that read like a name, not a run-on string.

A 45-year-old, family-owned screen printer that's grown into one of the largest in the Louisville area has several key pages — pricing, signs, and the sportswear catalog — indexed with the business name and city merged into one unbranded string. It's title-tag cleanup, not a redesign: same week to fix, and it makes every one of those pages read like a real business again.

What I'd Fix In The First 2-3 Weeks

  1. 01Fix the title template so pricing, signs and the sportswear catalog read "Sampan Screen Print — Jeffersonville" instead of a merged string.
  2. 02Add unique meta descriptions per service page (screen printing, embroidery, signs) instead of relying on one shared description.
  3. 03Add LocalBusiness schema naming the Jeffersonville address, the 1979 founding date, and the Louisville service area.
  4. 04Audit remaining catalog pages for the same merged-title pattern before it spreads to new product listings.
