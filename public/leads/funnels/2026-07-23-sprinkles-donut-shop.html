What I Found On Your Site

01
✗

Your original pages are indexed with no branding at all

/contact.html, /about.html, and /home.html show up on Google under bare titles - "Contact," "About," "Home" - with no mention of Sprinkles, donuts, or West Michigan anywhere in them. Someone searching your own business name could land on a listing that doesn't say who you are.

02
⚠

Newer pages use your branding — the old ones don't

Your Menu and Locations pages are indexed as "Menu | Sprinkles Donut Shop" and "Locations | Sprinkles Donut Shop" - correct and consistent. But the legacy .html pages sit right alongside them with none of that branding, so the site reads as two different eras stitched together.

03
⚠

Two different pages are both trying to be "Contact"

Google has both /contact.html (titled just "Contact") and a newer /contact page (titled "Locations | Sprinkles Donut Shop") indexed at the same time. That splits whatever search relevance either page earns instead of putting it all behind one clear, canonical page.

The Single Biggest Opportunity

One clean site, not two competing versions.

A family bakery that's grown from one Hudsonville shop into eight West Michigan locations is currently sending Google two different signals about who it is — legacy pages with no branding, and newer pages that do it right, plus two indexed pages both trying to be "Contact." Consolidating onto one consistent, correctly-titled site is cleanup work, not a rebuild.

What I'd Fix In The First 2-3 Weeks

  1. 01Rewrite the /contact.html, /about.html, and /home.html titles to match the branded format already used on your Menu and Locations pages.
  2. 02Redirect the old /contact.html page into the newer /contact page so Google indexes one canonical contact page instead of two.
  3. 03Add LocalBusiness schema for each of the 8 locations so Google has one clean, structured source of truth for hours and addresses.
  4. 04Write unique meta descriptions for each location page so every Hudsonville-to-Muskegon search shows the right address and hours, not a generic snippet.
