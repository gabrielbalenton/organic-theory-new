What This Kind Of Hire Usually Misses

01
✗

Workflows that break silently when an API changes

GA4, Search Console, SEMrush, WordPress, QuickBooks/NetSuite, and Microsoft 365 each have their own auth expiry and schema drift. Without monitoring on every connector, a token quietly expires and nobody notices for weeks — the automation just stops, or worse, keeps running on stale data.

02
✗

A single engineer, a single black box

You already have two automation owners — marketing and finance. Add a third generalist with no shared documentation standard and you have three black boxes, not one system. The moment any one of them is unavailable, that slice of automation stops being maintainable by anyone else.

03
⚠

Human-review gates that get skipped under time pressure

"A human reviews it before it goes public" is only as strong as whether it survives a deadline crunch. If the review step isn't built into the workflow architecture itself, it's the first thing that gets waved through when things get busy.

04
⚠

Ad-hoc scripts that don't survive the builder leaving

An internal process encoded as an undocumented Claude Skill or a one-off Python connector is tribal knowledge with extra steps. If the person who wrote it moves on, the "repeatable, documented system" your post asks for stops being either.
