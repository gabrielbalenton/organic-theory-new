Growth Audit — Beesley Monument & Vault Co

Five generations, and one of your pages doesn't even say "Beesley".

Richard and Nathan Beesley are carrying forward a business that's been honoring Provo families since 1889 — but a couple of gaps in how Google indexes the site are quietly working against that history.

What I Found On Your Site

01
✗

Your Burial Vaults page is indexed twice

Google has the current page at www.beesleymonument.com/burial-vaults indexed alongside an old, insecure leftover at http://beesleymonument.com/cemetery-vaults/ — different slug, no www, plain http instead of https. Two copies of the same page, competing with each other instead of one building authority.

02
✗

One indexed page doesn't carry your name at all

beesleymonument.com/core-page is indexed under the title "Memorial Services | Utah" — a leftover default page-builder slug and title. It's the only page across your entire indexed site that doesn't include "Beesley" anywhere, despite five generations and over 130 years behind the name.

03
⚠

Five generations of trust isn't leading the search snippet

For a decision this personal, "fifth-generation, family-run since 1889" is exactly the kind of detail that earns a click over a corporate monument dealer — but it's not what shows up first when a Provo family searches.
