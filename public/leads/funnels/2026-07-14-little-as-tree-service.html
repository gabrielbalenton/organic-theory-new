Growth Audit — Little A's Tree Service

130+ five-star reviews, and Google doesn't show a single one.

Little A's Tree Service has over 25 years in Greeneville and a reputation most tree companies would kill for — but none of it shows up as star ratings when someone actually searches for you.

What I Found On Your Site

01
✗

Your 130+ 5-star reviews are invisible in Google search

Your homepage proudly states "Over 130+ 5-Star Reviews" — but there's no review or rating schema markup telling Google to actually display that. When someone searches "tree service Greeneville TN," your listing shows no stars, no rating count, nothing — while the reputation you've spent 25 years building sits invisible.

02
✗

Nearly every photo on your site has no alt text

Your gallery — 30+ real before/after removal and trimming photos — has zero descriptive alt text on any image. That's a double loss: it's invisible to screen readers, and Google Image Search has nothing to index for "tree removal Greeneville TN" or "stump grinding before and after," searches where your actual work could be showing up.

03
⚠

No structured data for your service area

You list nine surrounding counties you serve in your footer, but there's no LocalBusiness or Service schema connecting Greg Cover's ISA certification, your hours, and your service area together for Google. That's a missed rich-result opportunity every competitor with proper markup gets for free.

04
⚠

Certifications are mentioned, not proven, in search

Greg being an ISA Certified Arborist is a real differentiator over unlicensed competitors, but it only lives in body copy — it's not marked up anywhere Google can surface it as a trust signal in a snippet.

What I'd Fix In The First Week

  1. 01Add Review + AggregateRating schema so your 130+ Google reviews show as star ratings in search.
  2. 02Write descriptive alt text for every gallery and service photo — 30+ images currently invisible to image search.
  3. 03Add LocalBusiness + Service schema covering your nine-county service area and business hours.
  4. 04Surface Greg's ISA certification as a marked-up credential, not just body copy.
  5. 05Set up a simple review-request flow so new jobs keep feeding your rating instead of relying on luck.
