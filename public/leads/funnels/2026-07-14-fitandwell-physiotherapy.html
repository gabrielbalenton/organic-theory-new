Growth Audit — FitandWell Physiotherapy

29 years of experience, and two small things undercut it.

FitandWell has a genuinely strong pitch — one-on-one sessions, 29 years of hands-on experience, an 8-step diagnostic process. But a duplicated word sits right in your main headline copy, and the email address in your header points to a different domain than the one people are actually reading.

What I Found On Your Site

01
✗

A repeated word in your main headline copy

The hero section reads "get your problem properly properly with the right treatment" — "properly" is duplicated back-to-back. It's right under your H1, the first real sentence a visitor reads, and it reads as unproofed rather than 29-years-experienced.

02
✗

Your contact email points to a different domain than your site

Every page shows hello@fitandwellphysio.com.au in the header — but the site itself lives at fitandwellphysio.com, with no ".au." Every internal link, the logo, and the booking system all point to the .com domain, so the .com.au in your own contact email is the one inconsistent detail on the page.

03
⚠

29 years of experience isn't in your title or meta description

Your current title is "Physiotherapy Treatments | Physical Therapy For Pain Management" — accurate, but generic enough to describe almost any physio clinic. The 29-years-experience detail and the one-on-one, full-session focus that actually differentiate you never make it into what Google shows searchers.

The Single Biggest Opportunity

Fix the duplicated word, reconcile the domain, and rewrite the title and meta description around 29 years of one-on-one experience.

Right now the copy and the contact details both read as slightly unreviewed, and Google has no reason to rank you above a bigger, more generic physio chain. Fixing the small stuff and leading with your real differentiators together is what moves the needle.
