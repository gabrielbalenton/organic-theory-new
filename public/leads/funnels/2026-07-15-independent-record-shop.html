Growth Audit — The Independent Record Shop

Your homepage is 9,620 pixels tall and never resizes.

You've built something genuinely special on Bath Road — press coverage, a loyal WhatsApp community, a monthly Vintage Fair. But the site itself was built as one giant fixed-size canvas, and most of the customers finding you are on a phone.

What I Found On Your Site

01
✗

The whole page is one fixed 1,120px-wide canvas

I checked your page's source code — every single element (text, photos, buttons) is pinned to a hardcoded pixel position on a canvas that's set to a minimum width of 1,120px and over 9,600px tall. There's no responsive layout underneath it at all, which means on a phone screen the whole page either gets squeezed unreadably small or forces horizontal scrolling.

02
✗

No meta description anywhere in your <head>

Your homepage's <head> only has a <title> tag — no meta description at all. Google has nothing to pull from when building your search snippet for "record shop Cheltenham," so it's guessing instead of showing something you'd actually pick.

03
⚠

Nearly every photo has blank alt text

I checked the image tags directly — your shop photos, event flyers and product shots all carry an empty alt attribute. Only the six small nav icons (Home, About, Contact) have real alt text, which tells me it's an export default rather than a deliberate choice. That's a missed accessibility and Google Image Search opportunity on every photo of the shop.

The Single Biggest Opportunity

Rebuild the site to actually resize on a phone, then add a real meta description and alt text on top.

Most people finding a record shop today are searching on their phone, mid-scroll, mid-commute. Right now that visitor lands on a page that either forces them to scroll sideways or renders unreadably small — before they ever get to see the press coverage, the Vintage Fair, or the Wants & Wanted list that make this shop worth the trip.
