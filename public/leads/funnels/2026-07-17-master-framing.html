Growth Audit — Master Framing

Your suburb pages are almost word-for-word identical.

Master Framing has quietly built a great local SEO idea — a dedicated page for Rose Bay, North Sydney, Double Bay, Crows Nest, Paddington, and more — but the pages read as the same paragraph with the suburb name swapped in, and that's exactly what Google flags as thin, near-duplicate content.

What I Found On Your Site

01
✗

Your "Areas We Serve" pages repeat the same line, suburb-swapped

Your North Sydney, Rose Bay, and Double Bay pages each say some version of "pickup and delivery to [suburb] and surrounding areas, plus courier delivery options" — same sentence, different suburb name. Google can treat pages like this as duplicate content and choose to rank only one or two of them, which quietly wastes the whole location-page strategy you already built.

02
⚠

Two separate pages for the same idea

You have both a "Locations" page and an "Areas We Serve" page — two different URLs competing for the same search intent instead of one strong page.

03
⚠

25 years of family-owned craftsmanship isn't the headline

You've been family-owned and operated in Bellevue Hill since 2000 — that's exactly the kind of trust signal that beats the big online framing chains, but it's buried on the About page instead of leading the homepage.
