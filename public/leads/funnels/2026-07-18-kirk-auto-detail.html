Growth Audit — Kirk Auto Detail LLC

Two pages are competing against each other.

20 years of hands-on detailing experience in the West End is real, hard-won trust. But your own site is splitting its search ranking between two pages instead of building on one.

What I Found On Your Site

01
✗

Two separate, indexed pages both cover exterior detailing

Google has both kirkautodetailllc.com/exterior ("Car Detailing | West End, PA") and /exterior-detailing ("Exterior Car Detailing | Pittsburgh, PA") indexed and live at the same time — two URLs targeting essentially the same service and keywords.

02
⚠

That splits your ranking signal instead of consolidating it

Every link, click, and bit of relevance for "exterior detailing Pittsburgh" gets divided between two pages instead of compounding on one — classic keyword cannibalization that quietly caps how high either page can rank.

03
⚠

There's a second location page for Carnegie, PA — but no clear hub

/contact/auto-detailing-carnegie-pa is indexed separately from your main /contact page, which is good for local reach, but without a clear internal linking structure it risks the same split-signal problem as the exterior pages.

The Single Biggest Opportunity

Stop your two exterior pages from competing with each other.

Consolidating /exterior and /exterior-detailing into one canonical page with a 301 redirect is a same-day fix that lets 20 years of real Pittsburgh reputation build toward one ranking instead of splitting it in two.

What I'd Fix In The First Week

  1. 01301 redirect /exterior into /exterior-detailing to consolidate ranking signal onto one page.
  2. 02Add internal links from the homepage and Carnegie contact page to the consolidated service pages.
  3. 03Add LocalBusiness/AutoDetailing schema so pricing, hours and service area surface as rich results.
  4. 04Rewrite meta descriptions per service page targeting real Pittsburgh/Carnegie search terms.
  5. 05Resubmit a clean sitemap and monitor Search Console for any remaining duplicate coverage.
