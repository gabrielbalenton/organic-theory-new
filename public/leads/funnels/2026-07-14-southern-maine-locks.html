Growth Audit — Southern Maine Locks

Your photos are telling Google the wrong story.

Southern Maine Locks has real local trust in Portland — a family-owned name and genuine five-star reviews — but the alt text behind your photos is still raw stock-image leftovers, and it's quietly working against you.

What I Found On Your Site

01
✗

Photo alt text is cut off mid-word

The image next to your Lockouts service reads "Emergency fire exit door. Closed up latc" — the word "latch" gets cut off. It's a small thing, but it's the raw, truncated caption from a stock photo library, never rewritten for your site.

02
✗

An exported filename is showing as alt text

Your Auto Keys photo's alt text literally reads "Hand of business man gives the car key_edited" — the "_edited" suffix is left over from the file export. None of your photos are eligible to show up in Google Image Search for "locksmith Portland Maine" the way they're written now.

03
⚠

Your nav and your services don't use the same names

The top navigation says "BUSINESS," but the page itself calls the same section "Commercial Locks." Small mismatches like that make a site feel like it was built in pieces rather than as one clear story.

04
⚠

No schema markup for your service area

You list sixteen towns you serve across Southern Maine, but there's no structured data telling Google that — so you're leaving local map-pack visibility on the table in Scarborough, Westbrook, and everywhere else on that list.
