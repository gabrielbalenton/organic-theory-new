Growth Audit — Merissa Leigh Salon

Your brand story lives on Instagram — not on your homepage.

Merissa Leigh Salon is building a real brand voice around elevated color, modern styling, and community — but none of that reaches merissaleigh.com. Right now it's a welcome video, a contact form, and an hours block, with nothing for Google — or a new client — to find.

What I Found On Your Site

01
✗

Your homepage says nothing about what makes you, you

"Elevated color," "modern styling," "community" — that's the brand you're building on social media. None of that language exists anywhere on merissaleigh.com today. A new visitor lands on a welcome video and has no idea what actually sets you apart.

02
✗

No service menu anywhere on the site

There's no list of what you actually offer — color, cut, styling, treatments — before someone has to leave the site and open Vagaro just to find out. That's friction most visitors won't push through.

03
⚠

No stylist bios or photos of real work

No names, no specialties, no single photo of a finished color or style on the homepage — even though that's exactly the content already being produced for Instagram and TikTok.

04
⚠

Weak SEO — generic "Welcome" framing

The page title and copy don't mention Greenwich, hair salon, or any service you offer. When someone searches "hair salon Greenwich CT," Google has nothing on your own page to match against.

What I'd Fix In The First 2 Weeks

  1. 01Rewrite the homepage hero and copy around your actual brand pillars — style, connection, community, elevated color.
  2. 02Add a real services section so visitors know what you offer before they ever open Vagaro.
  3. 03Write a title tag and meta description targeting "hair salon Greenwich CT" instead of a generic "Welcome" page.
  4. 04Add stylist bios and real photos of finished color and styling work.
  5. 05Add HairSalon/LocalBusiness schema markup so Google can show your hours, address, and reviews directly in search.
