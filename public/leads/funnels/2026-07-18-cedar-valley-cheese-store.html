What I Found On Your Site

01
✗

Your Contact page is indexed as "Organic Cheese"

Google shows cedarvalleycheesestore.com/contact-us/ with the title "Organic Cheese in Belgium, WI | Cedar Valley Cheese Store" — a title that describes a product category, not a way to reach you. It looks like every page on the site is getting the same "[Category] in Belgium, WI" title template regardless of what the page actually is, so a customer searching for your phone number or hours sees a cheese-category title instead.

02
⚠

Two separate shopping systems are both indexed

Google indexes both cedarvalleycheesestore.com/shop-all/ and cedarvalleycheesestore.com/online-cheese-store/gift-boxes/ on the main domain, plus a fully separate store.cedarvalleycheesestore.com subdomain — three different shopping paths a customer could land on, with no single one clearly established as the place to actually buy.

03
⚠

Winning the Wisconsin State Fair Seal of Excellence five times for your string cheese is exactly the kind of credential that makes someone choose you over a grocery-store cheese aisle — but it isn't in the title or meta description Google shows for your homepage.

Fix the mismatched titles, consolidate the shopping paths.

79 years and five State Fair awards is real proof — right now it's undercut by a Contact page titled "Organic Cheese" and three different places to shop. Fixing the title template and picking one canonical shopping path turns a confusing footprint into one that matches the trust you've actually built.

What I'd Fix In The First 2-3 Weeks

  1. 01Rewrite the Contact page title so it actually describes contacting you.

  3. 03Pick one canonical shopping path between /shop-all/, /online-cheese-store/, and the store subdomain.

  5. 05Add LocalBusiness schema so Google can show your hours and tasting schedule as rich results.
