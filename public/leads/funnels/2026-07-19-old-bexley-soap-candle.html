What I Found On Your Site

01
✗

Four pages are all targeting "beginners soap making course London"

/thecourses, /all-courses, /soap-making-for-beginners, and /thecourses/p/beginners-soap-making-course all show up independently in Google, each built around the same core search — your best-selling course. Instead of one strong page absorbing all the relevance and backlinks, four weaker pages are splitting it and competing against each other.

02
⚠

Google has to guess which page to rank — and often picks the weaker one

When multiple pages target the same phrase, search engines can't tell which one you actually want ranked, so ranking signal gets diluted across all of them instead of concentrating behind your strongest page.

03
⚠

A genuine award and Guild membership are buried in "About"

Highly Commended at the Bexley Business Excellence Awards and membership in the UK Guild of Craft Soap and Toiletry Makers are real, verifiable trust signals — right now they live in general About copy instead of being surfaced on the course pages where a first-time booker actually decides.

The Single Biggest Opportunity

One course page, not four competing ones.

Your beginners soap course — the one you sell out — is currently split across four separate indexed pages, each competing against the others for the exact same search. Consolidating them into one canonical page, with the award and Guild membership front and center, stops your own pages from undercutting each other.

What I'd Fix In The First Week

  1. 01Pick one canonical "beginners soap making course London" page and 301-redirect or canonicalize the other three into it.
  2. 02Surface the Bexley Business Excellence Award and Guild membership directly on that page, not buried in About.
  3. 03Cross-link the candle-making and one-to-one/business compliance pages from that hub so they reinforce it instead of competing with it.
  4. 04Add Course/Event schema so class dates and pricing can show as rich results.
  5. 05Resubmit the consolidated sitemap in Search Console so Google re-crawls the site as one clean structure.
