Growth Audit — Bicycle Recycle

Google thinks your homepage is called "HOME".

Bicycle Recycle has been Mark Johnson's family-run bike shop in Moorabbin for over 30 years — but your site's own code doesn't say "Bicycle Recycle," "bike," or "Moorabbin" anywhere in the one place Google looks first.

What I Found On Your Site

01
✗

Your homepage's title tag is literally "HOME"

I checked your page's source code — the <title> tag, the exact text Google shows in search results and browser tabs, just reads "HOME." No "Bicycle Recycle," no "bike shop," no "Moorabbin." For a shop that's been on South Road for 30 years, that's the single biggest reason you're not ranking for "bike shop Moorabbin."

02
✗

No meta description anywhere in your <head>

There's no meta description tag on your homepage at all — I checked the raw HTML directly. Google has nothing to pull from when it builds your search snippet, so it auto-generates one instead of showing something you'd actually choose to say about 30 years of bike sales, repairs and parts.

03
✗

Your "email us" icon still points to a placeholder address

The email icon in your hero section links to mymail@mailservice.com — an unedited template placeholder, not bicyclerecycle@bigpond.com, which does show up correctly further down in your footer. Anyone who clicks that top icon to email you is sending it nowhere.

04
⚠

Your social icons link to generic homepages, not your profiles

Facebook, Instagram, Twitter and YouTube icons all point to the bare platform homepage — facebook.com, instagram.com — instead of Bicycle Recycle's actual pages. Customers looking to follow along with new stock never make it there.

Fix the title tag, write a real meta description, and repair the broken contact links — in that order.
