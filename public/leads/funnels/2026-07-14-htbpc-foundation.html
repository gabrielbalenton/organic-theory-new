What This Kind Of Hire Usually Misses

01
✗

Thin technical SEO and no nonprofit schema

Small-staff nonprofit sites routinely skip NonProfit/Organization structured data and proper on-page SEO, which quietly caps how far awareness and program pages can rank against much larger health orgs.

02
✗

WordPress plugin bloat and security/backup debt

Sites maintained piecemeal over years tend to accumulate unused plugins, stale backups, and unpatched vulnerabilities — a real risk for a site handling donor information.

03
⚠

FluentCRM set up once, never segmented

It's common for CRM automations to get built once at launch and never revisited — one generic newsletter to everyone instead of separate flows for donors, supporters, and veteran outreach.

04
⚠

Inconsistent posting across five social platforms

Without a shared content calendar, Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn, YouTube, and X drift out of sync — awareness-month momentum builds, then goes quiet for weeks at a time.
