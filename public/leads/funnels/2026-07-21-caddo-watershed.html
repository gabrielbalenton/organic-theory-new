The Single Biggest Opportunity

Nine numbers turned into nine readable pages.

A canoe builder whose work is based on his own designs and antique Rushton patterns is currently showing up in Google as a set of unlabeled page numbers — ?page_id=19, ?page_id=23, ?page_id=106, and more. None of this needs new content — it's a single WordPress settings change plus redirects, and it's the kind of thing that turns an invisible URL into one Google (and a customer) can actually read.

What I'd Fix In The First 2-3 Weeks

  1. 01Switch WordPress permalinks from "Plain" to "Post name," with 301 redirects from every old ?page_id= address so nothing already linked or bookmarked breaks.
  2. 02Rename each page with a keyword-bearing slug — /hand-built-canoes/, /paddles/, /contact/ — instead of a number.
  3. 03Noindex the ?cat= and ?author= archive pages so search results point at your real service pages, not internal WordPress listings.
  4. 04Add descriptive page titles and meta descriptions naming Gordon, TX and "hand-built canoe," so each page can actually compete for a local search.
