Growth Audit — Bohndell Sails & Rigging

156 years of history, and Google can only find four pages of it.

Bohndell Sails has been rigging and sailmaking on the Maine coast since 1870 — through the Bohndell family, then the Chaces since 1979. That's a real story sailors trust. Right now your website isn't telling Google any of it.

What I Found On Your Site

01
✗

Your Services page title literally says "Services 1"

Right now the browser tab and Google's search result for that page read "Services 1 — Bohndell Sails & Rigging." That "1" is a leftover placeholder from when the page was built — and it's live in search results for anyone looking up sail repair or rigging work near Rockport.

02
✗

Your About page URL is still "/new-page"

The page that tells the Bohndell-to-Chace story — 1870 to today — is indexed at a default, never-renamed web address. It costs nothing to fix, but right now it's a signal to Google that the page was never finished.

03
⚠

Only four pages of your site are indexed at all

Home, About, Contact, and one Services page. New sails, sail repair, standing/running rigging, and off-season storage are all real, separate services you offer — but they're all crammed onto a single page competing for the same search terms instead of each ranking on its own.

04
⚠

No local keywords in your titles

Nothing on the indexed pages targets "sailmaker Rockport Maine" or "sail repair Penobscot Bay" directly — searches sailors in your own harbor are actually typing.

The Single Biggest Opportunity

Fix the leftover placeholder titles, then split one thin site into pages that can each rank.

156 years on the same stretch of coast is a real trust signal nobody else in the area can claim — right now the site doesn't say so anywhere Google can read it. Cleaning up the titles and URLs is a same-week fix; building out dedicated pages for New Sails, Repair, Rigging, and Storage is what turns four thin pages into four pages that can each win their own local search.

What I'd Fix In The First 2 Weeks

  1. 01Rewrite the "Services 1" and "/new-page" title tags and URLs — a same-day fix.
  2. 02Split the Services page into New Sails, Repair, Rigging, and Winter Storage — each targeting its own search.
  3. 03Write meta descriptions and titles that lead with "Rockport, ME" and "Penobscot Bay."
  4. 04Add LocalBusiness schema so Google can show hours, location, and services directly in results.
  5. 05Bring the 1870-to-today family story onto a properly named About page.
