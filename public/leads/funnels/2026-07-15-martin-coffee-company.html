Growth Audit — Martin Coffee Company

Your site can't decide how old Martin Coffee is.

You're North Florida's oldest specialty coffee roaster — family-owned since 1957, third generation, a real story. But your own homepage tells that story three different ways, and the online shop your "Shop Now!" button leads to barely gets mentioned anywhere else on the site.

What I Found On Your Site

01
✗

Three different ages for the same business, live on the same site

Your homepage hero reads "67 YEARS AND COUNTING." The About section right below it says "over 67 years of excellence... since 1957." The footer teaser near the bottom of that same homepage says you've been "servicing the Jacksonville Florida Community for 60+ years." And your About page frames the whole story around 1935, calling today "more than 80 years later." That's three different numbers for one fact, all live right now.

02
✗

Home, About, and Contact all share one meta description — and it skips your shop

I checked the metadata on all five main pages. Your Home, About Us, and Contact pages all use the exact same sentence word-for-word: "Martin Coffee Co. in Jacksonville, Florida services Offices, Coffee shops, Restaurants, and Hotels with their specialty coffee, machinery, and maintenance." Your Shop and Testimonials pages, by contrast, each have their own accurate description — proof the site can do it, it just wasn't done for three of your most-visited pages. None of the three even mention you sell coffee online.

03
⚠

Your posted hours don't match between your website and Facebook

Your own Contact page lists Monday-Friday, 7:30am-4pm, closed weekends. Your Facebook Business page — the one that shows up right alongside your Google listing — lists Monday-Thursday 7am-3pm and Friday 7am-2pm. A customer checking either source gets a different answer for when your door's actually open.

04
⚠

The homepage only speaks to wholesale buyers — not the people clicking "Shop Now"

Your "Services We Provide" section has exactly three cards: Restaurants & Hotels, Your Coffee Shop, Your Office. All wholesale. But the very first button in your hero is "Shop Now!," with free shipping on orders over $40 right underneath it — and there's no card, no line, nothing on the homepage that actually speaks to the home customer who just clicked it.

The Single Biggest Opportunity

Tell one consistent story about a 1957 family coffee roaster, and give the "Shop Now" customer a homepage that actually speaks to them.

Right now your site undercuts its own best asset — nearly 70 years of family history — by stating it three different ways, and it undersells your online shop by never mentioning it in the metadata Google actually reads. Fixing the consistency gap and the messaging gap together is what turns "oldest roaster in North Florida" into something both wholesale buyers and home customers actually see and trust.

What I'd Fix In The First 2 Weeks

  1. 01Pick one number and one story — "family-owned since 1957" — and make the hero, About section, footer teaser, and About page all say it the same way.
  2. 02Write three separate, accurate meta descriptions for Home, About, and Contact instead of the one duplicate — and mention the online shop in at least one of them.
  3. 03Reconcile your posted hours between martincoffee.com/contact and your Facebook Business Page so customers get the same answer everywhere.
  4. 04Add a fourth "Home & Individual Orders" card next to your three wholesale service cards, so the "Shop Now" click actually lands somewhere it's addressed.
  5. 05Pull your real Kerry B. and Marco P. testimonials up onto the homepage instead of leaving them only on the separate Testimonials page.
