What I Found On Your Site

✗

Most pages are indexed as insecure http://

Checking Google's index for aardvarkletterpress.com, Our Team, FAQ, The Process, Weddings, Art, History, and Contact are all showing as plain http:// URLs, while only the homepage and Services page are indexed as https://. That's a site that never fully moved to HTTPS.

02
✗

Browsers can flag most of those pages "Not Secure"

A bride checking your Weddings page, or a corporate client checking your History and Team pages before a big presentation order, is exactly the kind of visitor an insecure-connection warning scares off before they ever get to your work.

03
⚠

HTTPS is a small but real ranking signal — Google can only apply it to the couple of pages that are actually secured, not the rest of the site that isn't.

The Single Biggest Opportunity

Force HTTPS across every page, not just the homepage.

Right now most of aardvarkletterpress.com — including the pages couples and corporate clients actually click into before booking — loads over an insecure connection. A site-wide HTTPS redirect is the single highest-leverage fix here: it removes "Not Secure" warnings on the pages that matter most and lets Google apply its HTTPS ranking signal consistently across the whole site instead of two pages.

What I'd Fix In The First 2 Weeks

  1. 01Force a site-wide 301 redirect from http:// to https:// on every page, not just the homepage.
  2. 02Update internal links (nav, footer, Team, FAQ, Weddings, Art, History, Contact) to point to the https:// version directly.
  3. 03Resubmit the sitemap to Search Console so Google re-crawls every page as secure.
  4. 04Add LocalBusiness schema so the "Best in Los Angeles" credential and reviews can surface as rich results.
  5. 05Pull in and display real Google/Yelp reviews on the homepage.
