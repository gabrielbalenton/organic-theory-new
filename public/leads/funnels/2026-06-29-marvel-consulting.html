Your website only speaks to
one type of client

Marvel Consulting handles tax returns, BAS lodgements, small business accounting, payroll, property investment, and mortgage broking — but the only content on the site is about self-managed super funds. That means the hundreds of people in Melbourne's western suburbs searching for help with everything else find a competitor instead.

What I found on your site

  • ✗

    Your blog only talks about one niche topic

    All three published articles are about self-managed super funds — a topic relevant to a small slice of your potential clients. Meanwhile, someone searching "how do I reduce my tax bill as a small business owner" or "accountant for a new business in Footscray" or "do I need to lodge a BAS quarterly" finds nothing from Marvel Consulting. Google uses your content to decide who to send to your site. Right now, it can only send people interested in self-managed super — and misses everyone else.

  • ✗

    No search description on any page

    When your site appears in Google results, there's no description telling someone why they should click. Google fills the gap with a random sentence pulled from the page — often something irrelevant or mid-paragraph. You're relying on luck to get the click instead of a clear, purposeful line that says what you do and who you help.

  • ✗

    "Book Now" sends visitors off your site

    The booking button in your header takes visitors to a separate third-party page (marvelconsulting.as.me). For someone who's nearly ready to get in touch, that redirect creates doubt — it feels like leaving your business. An embedded booking widget keeps the experience on your site and converts at a noticeably higher rate.

What the opportunity actually looks like

Melbourne's western suburbs have a dense population of small business owners, tradies, and property investors — exactly the people Michael's firm is built to help. Most of them search Google before calling an accountant.

Publishing four to six articles answering the questions those people actually type into Google — things like "tax deductions for tradies in Victoria" or "do I need an accountant to set up a company in Melbourne" — would put Marvel Consulting in front of that audience at exactly the moment they need help. No ads, no cold calls.

Paired with clear search descriptions on each page and a booking flow that stays on-site, that content would start turning search traffic into enquiries consistently.

What I'd fix, in order

  1. Write 4–6 articles targeting the searches your ideal clients actually make (small business tax, BAS, property investment, new business setup)
  2. Add a clear, purposeful description to every page so Google shows the right snippet and more people click through
  3. Embed a booking widget directly on the site to remove the off-site redirect
  4. Add structured data so Google can surface your opening hours, location, and services in search results

Realistic outcome

Within 90 days: ranking for the searches your clients are already making, a consistent flow of inbound enquiries from people in Melbourne's western suburbs who are ready to hire an accountant — without spending anything on ads.
