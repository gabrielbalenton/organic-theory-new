Growth Audit — Ashby Jewellers

You're named after Ashby. Your website barely mentions it.

Ashby Jewellers has real, verifiable heritage — third-generation, family-run, over 160 years on Bath Street — but the search-facing copy on your own site never mentions Ashby-de-la-Zouch, the town the business is literally named after.

What I Found On Your Site

01
✗

Your homepage and About Us page share the exact same title tag

"Independent Jewellers Leicester & British Jewellers" is the <title> on your homepage — and it's word-for-word the same <title> on your About Us page. Google reads duplicate title tags as a sign the pages aren't meaningfully different, which can hold both back from ranking for their own separate searches.

02
⚠

Every page promotes "Leicester" — none mention Ashby-de-la-Zouch

Your real registered address — confirmed on your own Privacy Policy, Companies House, and Baume & Mercier's official retailer locator — is 5-7 Bath Street, Ashby-de-la-Zouch, Leicestershire. That's the town your name comes from, and it never appears in your titles or meta descriptions, which only ever say "Leicester."

03
✗

Your privacy policy and a major brand partner both link to a dead website

Section 4 of your own Privacy Policy lists "info@jewellersark.co.uk" as an opt-out contact — the one instance in the whole document that isn't "ashbyjewellers.co.uk." Baume & Mercier's official "Ashby Jewellers" retailer page does the same, sending customers to "Visit Store Site" → jewellersark.co.uk. I checked — that domain now just shows a blank server hosting-panel login screen, not your business.

The Single Biggest Opportunity

Give every page its own title, and put Ashby-de-la-Zouch back in your own copy.

You already have the trust signals independents need — three generations, 160+ years on Bath Street, authorised major-brand dealer status, and real five-star reviews. Right now none of that is backed by search-facing copy that's unique per page or that even names the town you're actually in. Fixing the duplicate titles, the missing location, and the dead legacy link together is what lets the real story show up in search.

What I'd Fix In The First Week

  1. 01Write a unique title tag for the About Us page so it stops duplicating the homepage.
  2. 02Rewrite titles and meta descriptions across the site to lead with "Ashby-de-la-Zouch" and "Leicestershire," not just "Leicester."
  3. 03Replace the dead "jewellersark.co.uk" reference in your Privacy Policy with your real domain.
  4. 04Flag the same dead-link issue to Baume & Mercier so their retailer locator sends customers to your real site.
  5. 05Add LocalBusiness + JewelryStore schema so Google can show your real address and reviews as rich results.
