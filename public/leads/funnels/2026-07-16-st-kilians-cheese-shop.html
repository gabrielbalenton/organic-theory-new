What I Found On Your Site

01
✗

Two separate live websites for the same shop

Google's index shows both stkilianscheeseshop.com — your current Shopify store — and an older WordPress installation at wp.stkilianscheeseshop.com, both titled "St. Kilian's Cheese Shop & Market." The old one is still crawlable and still showing content as far back as a May 2016 post, meaning it was never taken down or redirected when the shop moved to Shopify.

02
✗

Ranking authority is split, not combined

Every link, review, and year of history pointing at the old wp. subdomain does nothing to help the current store rank — and vice versa. Two half-strength profiles instead of one that reflects two decades in the Highlands properly.

03
⚠

Someone clicking through from search has no way of knowing which "St. Kilian's" is current — the working online store, or a stale WordPress leftover that can't take an order.

The Single Biggest Opportunity

Redirect the old WordPress site, and let 20+ years of history rank as one shop instead of two.

Right now stkilianscheeseshop.com and wp.stkilianscheeseshop.com are both live, both indexed, and both claiming to be St. Kilian's. Consolidating onto one canonical domain with a 301 redirect is the single highest-leverage fix here: it combines split ranking signal and removes any chance a customer lands on a dead, unshoppable version of the site.

What I'd Fix In The First 2 Weeks

  1. 01301-redirect every indexed page on wp.stkilianscheeseshop.com to its matching page on the current Shopify store.
  2. 02Request removal of the old subdomain's stale URLs from Google Search Console once redirects are live.
  3. 03Add LocalBusiness + Store schema markup so the consolidated history shows as rich results.
  4. 04Pull in and display real Google/Yelp reviews on the homepage.
  5. 05Submit an updated sitemap to speed up reindexing on the single canonical domain.
